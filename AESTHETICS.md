# Site Aesthetics Guide

This site should feel calm, crafted, and a little strange.

## What We Are Going For

- warm cream paper, not bright white
- editorial readability first, retro atmosphere second
- selective retro artifacts, not a full pixel operating system
- refined serif reading experience with properly typeset symbolic accents
- physics and chaos as atmosphere, not explanation
- tea as a small ritual cue, not a mascot
- cozy research notebook energy rather than arcade energy
- clear professional identity alongside thoughtful writing

## Hard Rules

- no placeholder copy or dead-end links
- no giant side rails or extra chrome without clear purpose
- no crude retro shortcuts that read as temporary
- no faux operating-system gimmicks that overpower the writing
- no pixel fonts for paragraphs or long-form reading
- blog text should feel editorial and quiet, never like a dashboard
- retro elements should frame the experience, not dominate it

## Text Budget

- homepage copy should be concise and credentialed
- long-form explanation belongs on About, Research, and Blog pages
- utility labels should stay short, uppercase, and sparse

## Library Stack

- Astro for the site shell
- React islands for interactive elements
- Motion for restrained motion choreography
- KaTeX for mathematical typography
- dotLottie for companion-grade character animation
- local brand icons and local fonts for reliability
- avoid mixing multiple unrelated retro systems on the same screen

## Visual Checks

- first impression should be professional, readable, and memorable
- desktop should feel like a composed editorial homepage
- mobile should preserve the same identity without becoming toy-like
- every decorative element should either support coziness, computation, tea, or nonlinear dynamics
